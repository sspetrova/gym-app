EXERCISE SCIENCE KNOWLEDGE:

RECOVERY RULES:
- Muscle needs 48h minimum recovery between sessions targeting the same group
- Cardio (running/cycling/football) causes systemic fatigue: reduce intensity 15-20%, avoid heavy leg work next day
- Swimming: upper body fatigue — avoid shoulder-heavy exercises next day
- Yoga: minimal fatigue, no restrictions
- Back-to-back gym days: reduce volume 30-40%, focus on antagonist muscles from yesterday
- Sleep quality matters: sleep 1-2/5 → treat like energy level 2, reduce intensity 20%

PROGRESSIVE OVERLOAD — THE CORE RULE:
- Same exercise 3x at same weight → +2.5kg next session, note in reasoning
- Same exercise 3x at same weight but reps dropped last time → keep weight, target full reps first
- Deload every 4-6 weeks (or when user rates sessions 1-2 twice in a row): reduce all weights 10%, keep same sets
- Never add weight AND sets at the same time — choose one

PROGRESSIVE SETS (BUILD-UP WITHIN A SESSION):
- Sets should NOT all be the same weight — always build up to the working weight
- 2 sets at 30kg: 27.5 → 30
- 3 sets at 30kg: 25 → 27.5 → 30
- 4 sets at 80kg: 70 → 75 → 77.5 → 80
- Increment: 2.5kg per step for <60kg, 5kg per step for ≥60kg
- The suggestedWeightKg is always the LAST (heaviest) set

INTENSITY BY ENERGY:
- Energy 5: 90-95% effort, heavy compounds first, 4-6 reps
- Energy 4: 85% effort, standard hypertrophy, 6-10 reps
- Energy 3: 75% effort, moderate, 10-12 reps, longer rest
- Energy 2: 65% effort, pump work, 12-15 reps, light weights, machines preferred
- Energy 1: Active recovery only — bodyweight, stretching, very light core work

DURATION → EXERCISE COUNT:
- 30 min: 3 exercises, 2 sets each
- 45 min: 3-4 exercises, 3 sets each
- 60 min: 4-5 exercises, 3-4 sets each
- 90 min: 5-6 exercises, 4 sets each

MUSCLE GROUP RECOVERY TIME:
- Chest/triceps: 48-72h
- Back/biceps: 48-72h
- Shoulders: 48h (they assist in most upper body work — be conservative)
- Legs/glutes: 48-72h
- Core: 24-48h (core recovers faster, can be trained more frequently)

INJURY SUBSTITUTIONS:
- Knee: no squats, no lunges, no leg press → hip_thrust, glute_bridge, upper body focus
- Lower back: no deadlifts, no barbell rows → cable_row, lat_pulldown, machines
- Shoulder: no overhead press, no upright rows, no pull-up → cable work below shoulder height, row variations
- Wrist: no barbell curls, no push-ups → cable or machine alternatives
- Hip: no hip thrusts, no split squats → leg_press, leg_extension, leg_curl
- Neck: no overhead press, no upright rows → seated or lying alternatives
- Elbow: no barbell curls, no skull crushers → dumbbell or cable light alternatives
- Ankle: no calf raises → upper body focus

SORENESS SCORING:
- 0: No soreness. Train normally.
- 1: Mild. Train normally, just warm up well.
- 2: Moderate. Reduce sets by 50% for that region. Focus on other muscle groups.
- 3: Severe. Skip that muscle group entirely. Do not train through severe soreness.

RECOVERY FEEDBACK APPLICATION:
- "great": User recovered well. Maintain or slightly increase load.
- "tough": Good session but close to limit. Maintain same weights, focus on form.
- "overworked": Too much. Reduce sets by 1 per exercise next session. Drop intensity 10%.
- "easy": Session was too light. Increase weight 5%, optionally add 1 set to main compound.

SESSION RATING APPLICATION:
- Rating 4-5: User is responding well. Keep or increase intensity.
- Rating 3: Average. Maintain current programming.
- Rating 1-2: Too hard, not enjoying, or feeling off. Reduce intensity. Consider shorter session. More machine work.
